Korok Seed Puzzles in Breath of the Wild

"Yahaha! You found me!" - A guide to finding those elusive Korok Seeds.

Explore the vast world of Hyrule and uncover its many secrets, including 900 hidden Korok Seeds!

What are Korok Seeds?

Korok Seeds are a collectible item in The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild. There are a staggering 900 of them scattered across Hyrule, hidden within various environmental puzzles. Collecting them allows you to expand your inventory slots by giving them to Hestu. While finding all 900 is a monumental task, uncovering even a few can be incredibly rewarding and add to the joy of exploration.

Here, we'll explore some of the most common and interesting types of Korok Seed puzzles you'll encounter on your journey!

Rock Circles/Patterns

Often, you'll find an incomplete circle, square, or other pattern of rocks. Your task is to find the missing rock nearby (it usually stands out) and place it in the correct spot to complete the pattern. Sometimes, it's a single rock that's out of place in an otherwise perfect pattern.

Sparkle Trails

Keep an eye out for a distinct trail of shimmering sparkles moving through the environment. Chase them down and interact with the source of the sparkles (usually a small, hidden Korok) before they disappear. This often requires quick movement or a good sense of direction.

Pinwheels

When you see a pinwheel, it indicates a target-based puzzle. This could involve shooting balloons, acorns, or even moving targets that appear after you stand near the pinwheel. Use your bow and arrow or other ranged attacks to hit the targets.

Block Puzzles

These puzzles involve two identical structures made of metal blocks. One structure will be missing a block, and you need to use your Magnesis Rune to find the missing block nearby and place it into the incomplete structure to mirror the complete one.

Water Lilies/Leaves

In bodies of water, you might spot a circle of lily pads or leaves. Simply dive or jump into the center of the circle to reveal the hidden Korok. Sometimes, you'll need to create a splash from a height.

Acorns/Pots

Look for small, breakable objects like acorns hanging from trees or hidden in crevices, or small pots. Shoot them with an arrow or break them with a weapon to reveal the Korok. Listen for a distinct sound that indicates their presence.

Tree Branches/Balloons

Sometimes, you'll find a single balloon tied to a tree branch or floating in the air. Shoot it with an arrow to pop it and reveal the Korok. These often require good aim and sometimes a bit of elevation.

Offering Puzzles

You might encounter statues or small bowls where an item is missing. Find the exact item (often an apple, a specific type of fruit, or a weapon) and place it in the empty spot to complete the offering and summon the Korok.

Boulder Rolling

Look for a large boulder positioned near a hole in the ground. Your goal is to roll or push the boulder into the hole. This often requires using Stasis, bombs, or even just pushing it manually down a slope.

Happy Hunting!

These are just some of the many creative ways Koroks hide in Hyrule. The joy of finding them often comes from observing your surroundings and experimenting with your runes and abilities. Keep an eye out for anything that seems a little "off" or out of place, and you'll be finding Korok Seeds in no time!

May your adventures in Breath of the Wild be filled with discovery!
